FLASH-BPM Simulation – System Description
=========================================

General Objective
-----------------
The goal of this project is to simulate the response of a detector system exposed
to high-energy charged particle beams using Monte Carlo simulations with OpenGATE.
The simulations aim to study energy deposition, detector response and dose profiles,
with potential applications in medical particle therapy and radiation monitoring.


Particle Beam
-------------
Particle type:
- Proton beam (initial studies)
- Possible future extension to carbon ions

Energy range:
- Minimum energy: 62.4 MeV
- Maximum energy: 252.7 MeV

Initial simulation strategy:
- Start with the extreme values of the energy range
- Later perform scans across intermediate energies

Beam spatial distribution:
- Initial model: Gaussian beam profile
- Other possible profiles may be tested in later studies


Detector System
---------------
Detector board (PCB):
- Material: FR4
- Thickness: 1.84 mm
- Function: support and readout electronics for the detector

Detector sensor:
- Current simulations use silicon as a simplified detector material
- Future studies may include other materials depending on detector design


Simulation Environment
----------------------
Simulation framework:
- OpenGATE (based on Geant4)

Physics models:
- QGSP_BIC_EMZ physics list

Transport medium:
- Air between source and detector

Additional geometry:
- Water phantom used to model tissue-equivalent material
- Used to study depth-dose profiles and Bragg peak behavior


Quantities of Interest
----------------------
The main physical quantities to be studied include:

- Energy deposition in the detector
- Dose distribution in the phantom
- Depth-dose profile (Bragg peak)
- Beam properties at the detector plane
  (energy, direction, position)


Outputs of the Simulation
-------------------------
The simulations produce several output data types:

- Phase-space files (particle properties at detector entrance)
- Energy deposition maps
- Dose distributions
